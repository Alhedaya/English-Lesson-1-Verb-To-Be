To be can be used as an auxiliary or a main verb. Am/Is/Are

Examples The verbs am / is / are are used with:-

  1. a noun group
  2. an adjective
  3. an expression of place or time
  4. an expression of age
  5. when forming the present continuous tense
  6. Question?
    1. Positive Answer + Yes
    2. Negative Answer - No

Am/Are

  • Are you an English teacher?
  • I am tired.
  • They are at home.
  • I am 44.
  • We Are learning English.
  • Am I disturbing you?
    • Yes you are. We are very busy.
    • No you're not. We are not very busy.

Is

  • Mr Bean is an English teacher.
  • She is English.
  • It is nine o'clock
  • He is two years old.
  • She is teaching English.
  • Is this your coat?
    • Yes, it is
    • No, it isn't
